Problem

  • Consolidation in the livestock industry has reduced competition and options for farmers.
  • Farmers have been forced from family to factory models of production, and open to contracted models of commerce.
  • Rural economies, animal welfare, human health, and the environment have all been impacted by these changes.
  • Public activism is needed to rebalance the ag economy and build a sustainable, productive future.

Meta-Problem

  • Public policy results are too often buried in PDF files and rarely-read glossy reports.
  • It has been too hard to tie analysis to activism and positive change.

Solution

Telling a story to motivate activism is crucial. Transitioning that story telling from the cramped confines of PDFs and Powerpoints to the dynamic possibilities of the web and low-barrier activism is the key to kickstarting viral loops via social networks.

HashMeat is an HTML 5 website powered by JQuery and Twitter that provides a framework for rapidly deploying relevant infographics while drastically reducing the friction for engaged citizens to challenge their representatives in government to affect change.

Using HTML 5's geolocation functionality, #HashMeat uses your latitude and longitude to identify your appropriate Senators and Representatives. With each unique infographic, #HashMeat allows the civic minded viewer to tweat context-sensitive tweets to government representatives, calling for change. Instrumental to HashMeat is the leaderboard (still under develoment) that visualizes the individual's influence via the use of a global leaderboard.
